Decoding the 2026 Core Math Syllabus Changes

YN
Yaw Ndaase Mensuoh
March 2026 • 5 min read

"Mathematics is not about numbers, equations, or algorithms: it is about understanding."

The West African Examinations Council (WAEC) has recently clarified the structural focus for the 2026 Core Mathematics examinations. For Ghanaian students, this means a shift in how topics are weighted and how "Show your work" marks are distributed.

1. The Rise of Numerical Reasoning

While algebra remains a cornerstone, the 2026 examiners are leaning heavily into Numerical Reasoning. This involves interpreting real-world data and applying mathematical models to predict outcomes.

// Example Change in Focus // Old Style: Solve for x in 2x + 4 = 12 New Style: A market vendor increases prices by 10%... find the net profit after tax.

2. Geometry & Trigonometry: 3D Visualization

Geometry marks are shifting toward 3D visualization. Expect more questions involving the intersection of planes and the calculation of surface areas for complex geometric solids. Our Vision platform's AI mock generator has already been updated to include these higher-order visualization problems.

3. The "Chief Examiner's" Trap

Every year, thousands of marks are lost not due to wrong answers, but due to poor expression of steps. In 2026, the marking scheme will penalize skipping intermediate algebraic steps more severely than before.
